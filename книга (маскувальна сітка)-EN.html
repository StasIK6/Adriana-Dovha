Camouflage Net

This photobook is a visual story about the making of camouflage nets, an attempt to show how much work and love is put into each one. Creating a camouflage net is a long and labor-intensive process. The fabric must be dyed in the appropriate color scheme, which depends on the location of the soldiers for whom the net is being made and the season of the year. Then, the fabric must be cut into narrow strips suitable for weaving. The most pleasant moment is rolling and packing the finished nets, because inside volunteers place children’s drawings with the best wishes for the defenders. After that, the nets travel East — to protect equipment and soldiers. The book shows how perfectly the nets blend into the surroundings. The volunteer center is located at the Lviv Academy of Arts, where nets have been woven every day since March 2022. The book shows the Kharkiv region. This photo story is a small puzzle piece in the great struggle of the Ukrainian people for their territory and independence.
